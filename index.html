Earned, Not Studied

Strategy that comes from
actually doing the work.

I studied mechanical engineering at Colorado State, but somewhere between thermodynamics and structural analysis, I realized I cared more about the "why" than the "how." Why does this product exist? Who needs it? What problem does it actually solve? That instinct pulled me toward product — and it's never let go.

Coming out of engineering school, I joined PwC and then Accenture, where I spent years embedded with product management teams helping them think through how to innovate and how to sustain growth through major strategic transitions. The MBA at Georgetown sharpened the business rigor. But both chapters — before and after Georgetown — were fundamentally about consulting. I loved the work. What I longed for was skin in the game.

So I crossed to the other side of the desk. At Gogo, GCI, CableLabs, and Calix, I stopped advising and started owning — roadmaps, P&L, team performance, market outcomes. Then at FreeWave, everything I had learned came together. I took a 30-year-old hardware company and helped it find genuine product-market fit with a new customer base, a new product-led operating model, and a new place in the market. Rebuilding the revenue model, the go-to-market motion, and the product architecture all at once — that's the work I've realized I'm exceptionally good at.

Across all of it, I've led diverse, cross-functional teams and global delivery organizations spanning four continents — building the kind of alignment, collaboration, and shared momentum that turns a strategy on a slide into outcomes your customers actually feel.

"Every company I've worked with has a strategy that looks great in a deck and stalls in execution. The gap is almost never the idea. It's the alignment, the org design, the go-to-market motion: the operational reality the strategy forgot to account for. The advice I bring is built around yours."

What It Is

Clarity before
you commit.

Most companies approaching a product pivot already have a strategic direction. The problem isn't the vision. It's that leadership doesn't yet have a shared, honest picture of the gap between where the business stands today and what the new model actually requires.

The Transition Diagnostic is a standalone 2-3 week engagement that delivers exactly that picture. It's the front end of the full Transition Audit program — available as an independent sprint for leadership teams that want to validate the scope of their challenge before committing to a full transformation engagement.

The output is a Current-State Diagnostic Report that gives you and your leadership team a factual, prioritized view of the gaps, assets, and risks standing between your current business and the one you're building. No theory. No frameworks for their own sake. Just clarity.

Companies that complete the Diagnostic and elect to continue into the Transition Audit have Phase 1 credit applied to the full program. The work carries forward directly — you don't pay twice for what you've already built.

The Problem

Most pivots don't fail because
the strategy is wrong.

They fail because the organization isn't rebuilt to execute it. The CEO sees the destination clearly. The board has approved the direction. Maybe there's even a prototype or early customers. But underneath the surface, the entire operational engine of the company — sales compensation, marketing messaging, support workflows, development priorities, even the org chart itself — is still optimized for the old business.

Leadership teams find themselves in an agonizing middle ground: too committed to turn back, but unable to accelerate because every function is pulling from a different playbook. The result is a slow, expensive bleed of resources, morale, and market timing.

What makes this particularly painful is the isolation. CEOs and CPOs navigating these transitions rarely have peers inside the organization who fully grasp the scope of what needs to change. Board members want progress updates, not vulnerability. Direct reports are simultaneously managing the old business and trying to build the new one, often with conflicting incentives.

The Transition Audit breaks that pattern — bringing structured diagnostic rigor, cross-functional strategy development, and the pattern recognition of someone who has led this exact transformation before.

What You'll Walk Away With

Six deliverables.
One complete strategy.

Upon completion, your organization will have everything needed to begin executing the transformation with clarity, alignment, and confidence.

01
Unified Transformation Roadmap

Prioritized initiatives, clear ownership, sequenced milestones, and approximately five KPIs that give your leadership team a shared scoreboard for measuring progress.

02
Redesigned Go-to-Market Strategy

Sales, marketing, customer success, and support — purpose-built for your new offering and operating model, not retrofitted from the old one.

03
Organizational Alignment Assessment

Specific structural recommendations on roles, reporting lines, and incentive alignment to eliminate friction between legacy operations and new-business execution.

04
Product Development Prioritization Framework

A framework that balances investment between legacy maintenance, transitional features, and new platform development — so engineering resources drive the pivot.

05
Legacy Asset Leverage Plan

Identifies which existing customers, products, partnerships, brand equity, and institutional knowledge are strategic accelerants — and a plan to extract maximum transitional value from each.

06
Executive Alignment & Shared Conviction

Built through facilitated workshops and collaborative strategy development — not a consultant's binder that sits on a shelf. Your team builds it with you.

Program Structure

Four phases.
Eight to ten weeks.

Companies entering from The Transition Diagnostic begin at Phase 2 — your diagnostic report and all materials carry forward directly, eliminating the discovery phase.

01
Phase One Weeks 1 to 2
Discovery & Diagnostic

For companies entering the Audit without first completing The Transition Diagnostic, this abbreviated phase establishes the current-state baseline needed to begin strategy architecture. Companies coming from the Diagnostic skip this phase entirely and enter at Phase 2.

Includes
  • Confidential 1:1 executive intake interviews with CEO, CPO, and 3-5 key leaders
  • Rapid current-state audit: go-to-market, org structure, product roadmap, and customer health
  • Sacred cow identification and legacy asset inventory
Deliverable
  • Working diagnostic baseline to anchor Phase 2
02
Phase Two Weeks 3 to 6
Strategy Architecture

Collaborative strategy development with your leadership team — building the transformation roadmap together so that alignment is baked in from the start, not bolted on at the end.

Includes
  • Go-to-market strategy redesign workshop across sales, marketing, customer success, and support
  • Organizational alignment sessions covering structure, roles, incentives, and capability gaps
  • Product development prioritization framework
  • Legacy product lifecycle planning including revenue protection and customer migration sequencing
  • Brand relevance assessment and positioning evolution
Deliverable
  • Draft Transformation Strategy with functional roadmaps for each area
03
Phase Three Weeks 7 to 8
Validation & Refinement

Stress-testing the strategy against financial realities, organizational constraints, and market dynamics — then refining it into an executable plan with built-in accountability.

Includes
  • Financial modeling of the transition including investment requirements and revenue bridge analysis
  • Risk assessment and mitigation planning for each strategic initiative
  • KPI framework development with approximately five leading and lagging indicators
  • Organizational change management planning and resistance mitigation
Deliverable
  • Refined Transformation Strategy with financial model, risk register, and KPI dashboard framework
04
Phase Four Week 9
Execution Blueprint & Handoff

Synthesizing everything into a single, prioritized execution plan your leadership team can begin implementing immediately — with clear ownership, sequencing, and milestones.

Includes
  • Executive alignment session with full leadership team to confirm buy-in and ownership
  • 90-day execution sprint plan with prioritized initiatives, assigned owners, and measurable milestones
  • Board-ready transformation narrative and supporting materials
  • Implementation guidance documentation and decision frameworks
Deliverable
  • The complete Transition Audit report — your board-ready transformation strategy and 90-day execution sprint plan
